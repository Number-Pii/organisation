# COO (Chief Operating Officer)

## Position Details
- **Department:** Executive Leadership / Operations
- **Reports To:** CEO
- **Direct Reports:** Head of Finance, Head of People & Talent, Operations Manager, Office/Facilities Manager
- **Employment Type:** Full-time

## Role Summary
The COO is the operational backbone, ensuring the company runs smoothly so other leaders can focus on building. They have scaled a company through rapid growth — not just an admin. They own finance coordination, HR, vendor management, and day-to-day operational excellence.

## Core Skills
- Operational strategy and execution
- Financial planning and budget management
- HR and people operations
- Vendor and contract management
- Process improvement and efficiency
- Risk management and compliance
- Cross-functional coordination
- Facilities and remote infrastructure management

## Project Involvement
| Project Type | Role in Project | Authority Level |
|---|---|---|
| Company Operations | Owner | Approve |
| Hiring Processes | Sponsor | Approve |
| Budget Planning | Lead | Approve |
| Vendor Contracts | Approver | Approve |
| Office / Remote Setup | Owner | Approve |
| Legal / Compliance | Coordinator | Approve |

## Approval Authority
- **Can approve:** Operational budgets, vendor contracts, HR policies, operational hires, process changes
- **Needs approval from:** CEO (executive hires, major budget decisions, legal matters)
